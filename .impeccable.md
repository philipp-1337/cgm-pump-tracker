# Design Context

## Users

Primarily Philipp — a person living with Type 1 diabetes who uses a Dexcom G7 CGM and Omnipod 5 insulin pump. Uses the tool daily, often while physically managing devices. May occasionally share with others who have diabetes. The context is personal health management: the tool is opened to answer a simple question ("when do I need to change what, and where should I put it?") and then closed.

## Brand Personality

**Quiet. Reliable. Yours.**

- Not a medical app, not a tech product — a personal health journal that happens to run in a browser.
- Warm and familiar, like a well-worn notebook, but not precious or decorative.
- Gives a sense of being in control, not being managed by the software.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **References**: Apple Health, Withings — clean, trustworthy, slightly clinical. The premium healthcare aesthetic: structured, high-contrast, no unnecessary decoration.
- **Anti-references**: Dark/neon crypto-portfolio aesthetic (the previous design), anything that looks "AI-generated" — glowing accents, gradient text, cyan-on-black.
- **Color**: Neutral, clean base (not warm parchment that dominates). Warmth through typography and small details only. Device colors (Dex blue `#1572a1`, Pod orange `#cc6f37`) are functional encodings, not decoration — use them purposefully and sparingly.
- **Typography**: Fraunces for headings at confident sizes. DM Mono strictly for data, timestamps, labels, and codes — never for body copy or chrome.
- **Theme**: Light mode only.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over personality** — The answer to "when do I change what?" should be visible in under 2 seconds. Hierarchy serves comprehension, not aesthetics.

2. **Neutral base, warm details** — The canvas is clean and close to white. Warmth lives in the serif typeface, rounded corners, and occasional tonal backgrounds — never in a saturated parchment wash that competes with content.

3. **Functional color** — Blue = Dex, Orange = Pod. Status colors (green/yellow/red) are meaningful signals, not decoration. Never add color just to be interesting.

4. **Earn every element** — No decorative gradients, glows, or backdrops that don't serve information. If it doesn't help the user understand something, remove it.

5. **Quiet confidence** — The interface is never alarming, never urgent-looking by default. Urgency only appears when a change is actually due. The default state should feel calm and in control.
